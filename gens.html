Gen Alpha
2010s-2020s

Researchers and popular media use the early 2010s as starting birth years and the mid-2020s as ending birth years. Most members of generation alpha are the children of Millenials. Generation Alpha is first to be born entirely in 21st century. Many members of generation Alpha have grown up using smartphones and tablets as part of their childhood. Being born into an environment where the use of electric devices is ubiquitous comes with its own challenges:
Cyber-bullying, screen addiction, and inappropriate contents etc.

Gen Z
1997-2012

Generation z (or simply Gen Z), colloquially known as zoomers. Generation Z refers to those where born between 1997-2012. Most members of Generation Z are the children of Generation X, but some are of Millenials. As the first social generation to have grown up with access to the internet and portable digital technology from a young age, members of Genration Z have been dubbed "didgital natives", even though they are not necessarily digitally literate.

Millenials
1981-1996

Also known as Generation Y (or simply Gen Y), generally define as people from 1981 to 1996. Most Millenials are the children of baby boomers and early Gen Xers. Millenials are often the parents of Generation Alpha. The generation is generally marked by elevated usage of and familiarity with the internet, mobile devices, and social media, which is why they are known for their adaptability and tech-savviness.

Genration X
1965-1980

This generation is being generally defined as people born from 1965 to 1980. Most members of Generation X are the children of the Silent Generation and early boomers. They are also often the parents of Millenials and Generation Z. They grew up during a time of significant technological and societal change, including the rise of personal computing and the end of the Cold War. Gen Xers have influenced work culture with their emphasis on flexibility and entrepreneurship.

Baby Boomers
1946-1964

This Genration is generally defined as people born from 1946 to 1964, during the post World War II baby boom. The baby boom has been described variously as a "shockwave" and as "the pig in the python". Baby boomers are often parents of late Gen Xers and Millenials. This generation experienced significant economic prosperity and social change. They are known for their Strong work ethic and loyalty.

The Silent Generation
1928-1945

This generation is generally defined as people born from 1928 to 1945. In the United States, the generation was comparatively ssmall because the Great Depression of the 1930s and World War II in the early to mid 1940s caused people to have fewer children. It includes most of those who fought during the Korean War. They are noted as forming the leadership of the civil rights movement as well as comprising the "silent majority".

The Greatest Generation
1901-1927

Also known as the G.I. Generation and the World War II Generation. This generation is generally defined as people born from 1901 to 1927. This generation is called greatest because, they were shaped by the Great Depression and were the primary participants in World War II. The Greatest Generation is renowned for their role in fighting and winning World War II.

The Lost Generation
1883-1900

In general, the Lost Generation is considered to be made up of individuals born between 1883 and 1900. It was the social generational cohert that came of age during World War I. "Lost" in the context refers to the "disoriented, wandering, directionless" spirit of many of the war's survivors in the early postwar period, disillusioned by the horrors of war.
